Best Full-Size Strollers

UPPAbaby Vista V3 ($1,100) — The reigning champion. The Vista V3 is an investment stroller that grows with your family. It accepts a bassinet (included), infant car seat (adapter required), or toddler seat. The reversible seat faces parent or forward. Add a RumbleSeat or PiggyBack board for a second child — no need to buy a double stroller.

The V3 improvements include a one-step fold, redesigned canopy with better ventilation, and improved suspension for smoother rides on Chicago sidewalks and lakefront paths. The massive under-seat basket holds a full diaper bag. One-hand recline and adjustable footrest make nap transitions easy.

Nuna MIXX Next ($850) — The best value premium stroller. Ring adapter included (no separate purchase for car seat compatibility). All-terrain tires handle everything from mall floors to park paths. The magnetic harness buckle is a standout feature — it clicks together with one hand while holding a squirming toddler.

Best Lightweight Strollers

Babyzen YOYO2 ($550) — The ultimate travel stroller at just 14 lbs. Folds to overhead bin size on most airlines. Despite its tiny footprint, it has a surprisingly smooth ride and accommodating seat for toddlers up to 40 lbs. Pop-up canopy provides good sun coverage.

UPPAbaby G-Luxe ($350) — One-hand fold, 16 lbs, deep recline for naps. The G-Luxe hits the sweet spot between lightweight convenience and full-size comfort. Great as a second stroller for travel and errands.

Best Jogging Strollers

BOB Wayfinder ($500) — The gold standard for running parents. 12-inch air-filled tires absorb bumps and cracks. Adjustable suspension handles different terrains. Hand-activated rear drum brakes for downhill control. Lockable swivel front wheel for jogging or unlocked for everyday maneuverability.

The Wayfinder improves on the classic BOB design with a one-hand quick fold, lighter weight (28 lbs vs 32 lbs for the previous Revolution), and improved handlebar adjustment. Compatible with most infant car seats via adapter.

Best Double Strollers

UPPAbaby Vista V3 + RumbleSeat ($1,500 total) — Convert your Vista into a double stroller by adding the RumbleSeat underneath. Both seats face forward, rearward, or one each direction. This is far more maneuverable than a side-by-side double stroller and fits through standard doorways.

Bugaboo Donkey 5 Duo ($1,800) — The only premium side-by-side that narrows to single width when you remove one seat. Each child gets an equal-sized seat with independent recline. Ideal for twins or Irish twins (siblings close in age).

How to Choose the Right Stroller

  • Newborn compatibility: If buying before baby arrives, ensure the stroller accepts a bassinet or infant car seat. Not all strollers are safe for newborns without these accessories
  • Fold type: One-hand fold is essential if you will be folding while holding a baby. Standing fold is convenient for hallway storage in apartments
  • Weight matters: If you live in a walk-up apartment or take public transit (CTA/L train in Chicago), every pound counts. Consider a lightweight stroller as your primary or secondary option
  • Test drive: Visit a store and push the stroller around. Check one-hand steering, fold mechanism, harness ease, and basket access. What feels intuitive varies by person
  • Resale value: UPPAbaby and Bugaboo hold resale value best — expect 50-70% return on Facebook Marketplace. Budget brands lose value quickly

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best stroller for newborns?

The UPPAbaby Vista V3 with included bassinet is the best stroller for newborns. The bassinet is safe for overnight sleep and clicks directly into the stroller frame. Alternatively, use any compatible stroller with an infant car seat adapter.

How much should I spend on a stroller?

Plan to spend $300-$600 for a quality stroller that lasts. Premium options ($800-$1,400) offer better build quality, smoother ride, and higher resale value. Budget strollers under $200 often need replacing within a year.

When can babies sit in a stroller without a car seat?

Most babies can use a stroller seat around 6 months when they have head and neck control. Before that, use a bassinet attachment or infant car seat adapter. Check your specific stroller manual for age and weight guidelines.

Is the UPPAbaby Vista worth the price?

Yes for most families. The included bassinet ($200 value), expandability to double stroller, excellent resale value, and 3+ year usable life make it cost-effective despite the high upfront price. If budget is tight, the Nuna MIXX offers 90% of the features at $250 less.

Can I take a stroller on an airplane?

You can gate-check any stroller for free on all major airlines. The Babyzen YOYO2 fits in overhead bins and qualifies as your carry-on. For gate-checking, use a stroller travel bag to prevent damage.
